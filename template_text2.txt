Integrated Campus Medical Facility & Patient Management System
A Full -Stack MERN Application for Smart Campus Healthcare AdministrationAtul Yadav | Roll No: 2201041
IIIT Guwahati | CS410 Project
◆Summary / Abstract
 The Integrated Campus Medical Facility & Patient 
Management  .System is a full -stack MERN web application that 
digitizes campus healthcare services. It replaces manual dispensary 
workflows with a secure role -based platform for managing 
appointments, health records, prescriptions, medicine inventory, 
emergency alerts, and notifications, improving accessibility, 
transparency, security, and operational efficiency.
Problem Solved
Paper -based medical records are hard to search, update, audit, and protect.
• Manual medicine stock tracking can cause shortage, expiry, and mismatch issues.
• Students do not get quick visibility of appointments, prescriptions, and health 
history.
• Emergency communication through notice boards or informal chats is slow and 
unreliable.
• Dispensary staff spend extra time on repeated administrative work instead of 
patient care.
Objective / Key Goals
To develop a secure, scalable, and role -based healthcare platform that 
modernizes campus dispensary operations through centralized digital 
records, automated inventory tracking, and structured communication.
Key Goals:
• Separate dashboards for Student, Doctor, Staff, and Admin.
• Digitize health records, appointments, prescriptions, medicines, and 
notifications.
• Use secure authentication and role -based authorization.
• Provide faster emergency alert delivery and real -time updates.
• Build a maintainable architecture that can be extended for mobile app, 
analytics, and telemedicine.

Method / Approach
The system follows a MERN -based three -tier architecture for better scalability, 
maintainability, and security.
Frontend Layer:
React.js, Vite, Tailwind CSS, React Router, Context API, and Axios provide 
responsive role -based interfaces.
Backend Layer:
Node.js and Express.js expose REST APIs for auth, facilities, medicines, 
hospitals, notifications, gallery, history, appointments, emergency alerts, and 
admin operations.
▣System Architecture
React.js Frontend  •  Role -based dashboards
Node.js + Express APIs  •  Business logic + routes
JWT + RBAC Security  •  Protected communication
MongoDB Atlas  •  Users, records, medicines, alerts
Database Layer:
MongoDB Atlas stores users, health records, appointments, medicines, 
notifications, gallery data, and admin -related records. Mongoose handles 
connectivity and schema management.

✓Results / Findings
The project delivers a production -ready, role -based campus healthcare 
platform with secure login, OTP verification, digital health records, 
prescriptions, inventory tracking, appointments, nearby hospitals, emergency 
alerts, notifications, admin analytics, and gallery management.
Its modular REST API backend centralizes all dispensary workflows into one 
secure, maintainable, scalable, and easy -to-extend digital system.
★Key Outcomes

• Secure multi -role access for Student, Staff, Doctor, Admin
• Digitized records, prescriptions, inventory, alerts and notifications
• Reduced paperwork and improved operational transparency
